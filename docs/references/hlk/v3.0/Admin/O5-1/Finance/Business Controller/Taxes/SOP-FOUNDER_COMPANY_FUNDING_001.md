1.0 Description

This document defines the approved path for moving value from a founder to a newly created or early-stage company in a controlled way. It is intended to reduce ambiguity around capitalization, shareholder loans, reimbursements, and other early-operating funding patterns that frequently arise before revenue is stable.

The procedure ensures that legal, accounting, and tax treatment are aligned before founder funds are mixed with company operations. It also creates an auditable record of why a given path was selected.

2.0 Purpose

* To standardize the early-stage funding relationship between founder and company.  
* To distinguish capital contribution, shareholder loan, and reimbursement paths.  
* To require supporting evidence and review before founder-funded costs are normalized.  
* To improve runway visibility and reduce accounting clean-up later.

3.0 Scope

This SOP applies to founder-funded operating activity, capitalization decisions, and related evidence capture for early-stage entities. It covers pre-revenue and low-revenue operating phases where founder resources may be used to support the company. It does not replace accountant sign-off on jurisdiction-specific tax treatment.

4.0 Procedure

4.1. Operating Cost Baseline

* **Trigger:** A new or early-stage entity expects founder-funded operating costs or low initial capitalization.  
* **Action:** Business Controller records baseline operating costs, including infrastructure, software, AI, data, subscriptions, and any recurring founder-funded expenses needed to keep the company operational.  
* **Output:** A baseline monthly operating-cost snapshot.

4.2. Funding Path Selection

* **Trigger:** The operating-cost baseline is ready and a funding decision must be made.  
* **Action:** Business Controller compares the available paths: capital contribution, shareholder loan, reimbursement, or another accountant-approved route.  
* **Action:** The selected path is documented with rationale, timing, cash impact, and operational constraints.  
* **Output:** A chosen founder-to-company funding path with approval rationale.

4.3. Evidence and Documentation Control

* **Trigger:** A funding path has been selected.  
* **Action:** Supporting evidence is captured, including invoices, expense records, transfer evidence, asset lists, founder approvals, and any required agreements.  
* **Action:** In-kind and cash support are kept distinguishable in the record.  
* **Output:** A controlled evidence pack for accounting and legal review.

4.4. Accounting and Tax Validation

* **Trigger:** Evidence is ready for operational use.  
* **Action:** Taxes reviews the proposed treatment and flags any local-law, filing, or documentary requirements before the path is treated as standard operating practice.  
* **Action:** Where accountant sign-off is required, the item remains provisional until that sign-off is recorded.  
* **Output:** A validated or blocked funding-treatment decision.

4.5. Runway and Review Cycle

* **Trigger:** Founder-to-company funding is active.  
* **Action:** Business Controller tracks runway, usage pattern, and whether the chosen path is still fit for purpose.  
* **Action:** Repeated ad-hoc founder support is treated as a signal that capitalization or operating model assumptions must be revisited.  
* **Output:** A periodic runway and treatment review.

5.0 Roles and Responsibilities

* **Business Controller:** Owns the process, funding-path recommendation, runway logic, and evidence completeness.  
* **Taxes:** Reviews local-law treatment, documentation sufficiency, and filing implications.  
* **Financial Controller:** Confirms that accepted treatment can be reflected cleanly in the accounting record.  
* **Founder / Entity Sponsor:** Approves the path and supplies source evidence for founder-funded activity.

6.0 Addendum

A.1: Funding Path Decision Matrix

* Capital contribution  
* Shareholder loan  
* Expense reimbursement  
* In-kind contribution  
* Deferred operating support pending accountant review

A.2: Minimum Evidence Pack

* Cost baseline  
* Payment or transfer evidence  
* Asset list where applicable  
* Founder approval or instruction  
* Accountant or tax-review note  
* Review date and next checkpoint

1.0 Description

This document defines how HLK prepares a founder-led entity for ENISA startup certification by assembling a controlled evidence pack before submission. The procedure separates eligibility, innovation proof, scalability proof, and governance evidence so that startup-law work does not rely on unstructured verbal claims.

The goal is to make startup-certification readiness explicit before external submission. The process begins before submission and can begin before the company is fully ready to file, provided the evidence pack is clearly marked as draft or provisional where needed.

2.0 Purpose

* To standardize how ENISA readiness is assessed and documented.  
* To distinguish formal eligibility checks from narrative claims about innovation and scalability.  
* To ensure that legal, founder, and operating assumptions are recorded before submission.  
* To maintain a controlled evidence pack that can be updated as the company evolves.

3.0 Scope

This SOP applies to startup-certification readiness work owned by Compliance for founder-led entities seeking ENISA recognition under the Spanish startup-law framework. It covers evidence assembly, pre-checking, and submission readiness. It does not guarantee certification and does not replace final legal or tax review.

4.0 Procedure

4.1. Eligibility Pre-Check

* **Trigger:** A founder or entity sponsor intends to pursue ENISA startup certification.  
* **Action:** Compliance records the baseline eligibility assumptions, including entity age, Spain nexus, turnover ceiling, dividend posture, non-listed status, and dependency on company-formation status.  
* **Action:** Any missing prerequisite is logged as a blocker rather than buried inside narrative drafting.  
* **Output:** An eligibility status of ready, partial, or blocked.

4.2. Innovation Evidence Assembly

* **Trigger:** Eligibility is at least partially understood.  
* **Action:** Compliance and Research assemble the proof that the venture is solving a meaningful problem through a new or materially improved product, service, or process.  
* **Action:** Technical sophistication alone is not accepted as sufficient; the evidence must explain why the innovation matters commercially or operationally.  
* **Output:** An innovation-evidence section with supporting references.

4.3. Scalability Evidence Assembly

* **Trigger:** Innovation evidence exists in usable draft form.  
* **Action:** Compliance captures the scaling logic, including repeatability, market expansion path, commercial leverage, operating model, and the elements that make the business more than bespoke consulting.  
* **Action:** Where the company mixes consultancy, research, and product/lab activity, the scaling narrative is made explicit rather than implied.  
* **Output:** A scalability-evidence section with clear assumptions.

4.4. Legal, Governance, and Operating Evidence

* **Trigger:** Innovation and scalability sections are in progress.  
* **Action:** Compliance collects the legal and operating prerequisites that support the submission, including founder/director standing, tax and social-security posture where required, and any company-formation facts needed for the file.  
* **Action:** Dependencies that still require external adviser confirmation remain flagged as pending.  
* **Output:** A governance-and-operating evidence section with unresolved items visibly tracked.

4.5. Submission Readiness and Change Control

* **Trigger:** The evidence pack is materially complete.  
* **Action:** Compliance reviews the pack for internal consistency and marks each item as validated, provisional, or unresolved.  
* **Action:** Material changes to the business model, legal setup, or founder posture after this point require a controlled update to the pack rather than an informal note.  
* **Output:** A submission-readiness determination and maintained evidence pack.

5.0 Roles and Responsibilities

* **Compliance:** Owns the readiness process, blocker tracking, and evidence-pack integrity.  
* **Holistik Researcher:** Supports innovation and scalability narrative quality where research framing is material.  
* **Legal Counsel:** Confirms legal prerequisites and entity-formation assumptions where required.  
* **Business Controller:** Supports turnover, operating model, and commercial-scaling evidence.  
* **Founder / Entity Sponsor:** Supplies business facts, supporting material, and approvals.

6.0 Addendum

A.1: Evidence Checklist

* Eligibility prerequisites  
* Innovation proof  
* Scalability proof  
* Spain nexus proof  
* Corporate/legal status evidence  
* Founder/director standing checks  
* Open issues log

A.2: Official Reference Set

* ENISA certification requirements  
* Plataforma Pyme startup-certification guidance  
* Ley 28/2022 and any later implementing criteria relevant to certification  
* Internal legal and finance notes required to complete the pack

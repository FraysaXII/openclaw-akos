## Innovation Case

### Core Innovation Claim

The venture combines corporate intelligence, business engineering, and a technology-enabled knowledge architecture to make organizational analysis more structured, repeatable, and actionable than traditional consulting.

### Working Innovation Narrative

The company is not merely selling expert opinion. It is building a structured operating method in which:

- business architecture and process knowledge are captured systematically
- AI can read and work across that architecture
- human research, intelligence, and structured methodology are combined with software-enabled delivery
- the underlying stack supports future productization rather than remaining permanently manual

### Stable Supporting Signals

- the founder described a technology base in place since 2023
- the operating concept includes connecting agents to business tools and knowledge sources
- the founder already has a documented architecture and operating corpus large enough to support structured delivery
- external interest already exists from at least one commercial prospect asking for software-oriented extension of the approach

### Evidence To Attach

- architecture overview
- methodology summary
- current stack map
- productization roadmap
- examples of technology-enabled delivery
- any client or prospect evidence showing demand for the approach

## Scalability Case

### Core Scalability Claim

The business has a credible path from founder-led delivery into repeatable, technology-enabled, and eventually productized service components.

### Why The Case Is More Than Bespoke Consulting

The model is not only "I advise companies." It has several scaling signals:

- the methodology is designed to be reused across departments and clients
- the architecture and documentation layer create a reusable delivery substrate
- the technology stack reduces the dependence on manual interpretation alone
- the same logic can support research, analysis, and later product/lab outputs
- market pull already points toward reusable software or platform-style extensions

### Scalability Risk To Control

The biggest risk is being framed externally as generic consultancy. If that happens, the technology-enabled and reusable layer can become invisible to reviewers.

### Evidence To Attach

- repeatable methodology steps
- modular service package structure
- examples of reusable AI-enabled workflows
- growth path from service to productized delivery
- commercial roadmap showing leverage beyond one-off engagements

## External party operating model (startup certification path)

- **Collaborative plan**: the external adviser develops the startup business plan **with** Holistika; Holistika remains the authority on facts, market, and operations.
- **Joint readiness gate**: filing occurs only when both sides agree the plan is ready.
- **Certification authority**: the competent body has final, partly discretionary judgment; adviser input does not guarantee an outcome.
- **Cycle time**: expect a long research-and-iteration cycle; this is normal for substantive plans.
- **Single mercantil**: keep **one** mercantil and **one** coherent narrative for certification and external positioning (aligned with [Research vs Tech Lab rationale](../../Research/RESEARCH_VS_TECH_LAB_ENTITY_RATIONALE_2026-04.md)).

## CNAE and mercantil coherence (banking channel)

- **Separate concerns**: startup certification narrative (this pack) is **not** the same artifact as **CNAE / IAE** selection with the banking incorporation channel—but they must **tell the same story** about what the company does.
- **Risk**: mismatch between activity description, CNAE, and certification narrative weakens filings (historical lesson: plan text vs registry activity codes must be reconciled early).
- **Operational bridge**: supply a **short activity description** and market context to the banking channel; the external adviser may help translate market context for the bank desk **without** becoming the legal selector of CNAE codes (codespace is broad and taxonomy lags technology-heavy activities).

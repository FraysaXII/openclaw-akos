## 7. Audience FAQ

### Investor

**Is this a consulting company or a technology company?**  
Both, by design. Services fund and validate the method; the technology lab turns repeatable parts of the method into software.

**Where is the founder proof?**  
Prior company-building work includes a multi-location franchise model with external financing and repeatable operations. Supporting detail is available under NDA where appropriate.

**What is the moat?**  
The moat is the operating loop: structured research, service delivery, internal software, brand governance, and repeatable client handoff all reinforcing one another.

### Customer-SME

**What will I actually get?**  
A scoped engagement with clear outputs: discovery, operating model, process design, technical implementation where needed, and handoff.

**Will this become an endless consulting dependency?**  
No. Engagements are designed to transfer capability. The goal is not to make the client dependent; it is to make the client more operationally capable.

**Where is the technological component?**  
Holistika operates an internal technology lab that builds and validates agentic knowledge systems, workflow tooling, and product infrastructure connected to the company's service work.

**Why is the founder credible for this scope?**  
The founder combines prior company-building, business operations, structured research, and technical implementation experience.

### Advisor

**What does Faycal need from me?**  
Usually one of four things: challenge a decision, validate a risk, review a document, or open a specific door. The best advisory input is precise and actionable.

### Partner

**Will Holistika compete with us?**  
The partnership fit depends on scope. Holistika is strongest where it complements a partner's commercial access, implementation capacity, or domain expertise with research, operating-system design, and technical delivery.

### Recruiter / Candidate

**What kind of person fits here?**  
Someone who can work with ambiguity but does not romanticize it; someone who writes clearly, documents decisions, and wants ownership of systems that will be reused.

# Founder Entity Formation Decision Memo

**Document owner**: Legal Counsel  
**Version**: 1.0  
**Date**: 2026-04-08  
**Status**: Final

---

## Executive Position

The recommended first entity should be framed as a corporate intelligence and business engineering company with a technology-enabled, scalable methodology and a future productization path. The immediate operating need is broader than pure software commercialization, so the first legal entity should not be forced into a narrow product-only frame. At the same time, it should not be described as generic consultancy if that weakens the later startup-certification story.

The current best-fit posture is:

- entity 1 captures research, intelligence, architecture analysis, and business engineering services
- entity 1 acknowledges a technology base and future productization path
- separate Tech Lab commercialization remains a future option rather than an immediate legal requirement

## Current Recommendation

### Recommended Legal Story

Entity 1 should be described as the operating vehicle for:

- corporate intelligence
- research-driven business engineering
- process and architecture analysis
- technology-enabled service delivery
- future productization or laboratory spinout where justified later

This framing is strong because it matches the founder's present revenue reality while preserving a credible innovation-and-scale narrative for ENISA.

### Recommended First Entity

Use a single founder-led entity first rather than creating a full multi-entity structure at launch.

Reasoning:

- current work still appears founder-led and service-heavy
- research and technology remain strategically linked
- premature structural complexity increases legal and administrative load
- the company still needs a clean operating baseline before a holding or spinout structure becomes worthwhile

## Incorporation Route Position

### Default Recommendation

Prefer the route that preserves legal fidelity over the route that merely maximizes speed.

That means:

- use a fast standardized path only if the resulting `object social`, activity description, and later startup-certification posture remain acceptable in writing
- otherwise prefer a more tailored route that protects the intended company story from day 1

### Why This Matters

The founder case is not a vanilla freelancer or commodity consulting setup. The company must support:

- broad research and advisory capability
- technology-enabled delivery
- possible future lab/product line separation
- a potentially stronger ENISA narrative than a standard consultancy shell would allow

If a standardized incorporation path cannot express that well enough, speed is the wrong optimization.

## What Should Live Inside Entity 1

### Include

- research and intelligence work
- business engineering and architecture analysis
- AI-assisted document and system interpretation as a delivery enabler
- current service revenue and founder-led engagements
- an explicit future productization option

### Do Not Force In Prematurely

- a separate holding logic
- a fully independent Tech Lab entity if there is not yet a clean revenue, cap-table, or liability reason
- product-only positioning that understates the current service and research layer
- a naming or filing strategy that assumes the entity name and the product/lab brand are already the same thing

## Decision Rationale

This recommendation is based on the following stable facts from the source synthesis:

- the founder already operates a broad research and architecture-heavy practice
- the technology layer is real and material, but it is still part of a broader offer
- startup certification is desirable, but it is a second-stage benefit rather than the legal act of incorporation itself
- future structural separation between Research and Tech Lab is plausible, but not yet mandatory

## Open Legal Dependencies

These points still require written confirmation from external advisers or final legal drafting:

- the exact `object social`
- the proposed `CNAE` package
- whether a standardized fast route fully preserves the intended framing
- the exact implications of current and future pluriactivity on founder status
- how future holding or spinout plans constrain the initial deed, if at all

## Change Triggers

This memo must be reviewed if any of the following becomes true:

- the company shifts from service-led to product-led revenue
- a co-founder or investor enters with structural implications
- a separate Tech Lab begins generating independent revenue
- the founder decides to pursue a holding structure in the near term
- external counsel recommends a materially different legal framing

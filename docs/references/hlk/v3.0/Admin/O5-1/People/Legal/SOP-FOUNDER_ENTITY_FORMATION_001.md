1.0 Description

This document defines the legal-readiness process for founder-led entity creation inside the HLK governance model. It standardizes how Legal Counsel frames the purpose of a new entity, evaluates the incorporation route, captures the proposed `object social` and related activity classifications, and prepares the founder for signature and downstream compliance work.

The procedure exists to prevent a fast but weak incorporation outcome. The goal is to ensure that the first legal entity matches the intended business model, funding path, startup-law trajectory, and future brand/IP posture before binding documents are executed.

2.0 Purpose

* To provide a repeatable legal-readiness workflow before company formation.  
* To ensure entity purpose, incorporation route, and documentation are aligned before signature.  
* To surface unresolved legal dependencies early enough for founder decision-making.  
* To create a clean handoff from incorporation into accounting, startup certification, and trademark/IP work.

3.0 Scope

This SOP applies to founder-led entity formation work owned by Legal Counsel where the company purpose, activity framing, or future startup-certification path materially affects the legal setup. It covers pre-incorporation readiness, route selection, legal-review checkpoints, and handoff. It does not replace jurisdiction-specific legal advice from licensed counsel or notarial requirements.

4.0 Procedure

4.1. Entity Intent Framing

* **Trigger:** A founder requests support to create a new entity, spinout, or first operating company.  
* **Action:** Legal Counsel captures the primary business intent in one clear sentence, including whether the entity is being framed primarily as research, consultancy, product/lab vehicle, or a defined hybrid.  
* **Action:** Legal Counsel records the intended services, expected revenue model, Spain nexus, expected operating footprint, and any future holding or multi-entity constraints already known.  
* **Output:** A concise entity-intent summary that can be reused in later route, tax, and certification decisions.

4.2. Incorporation Route Evaluation

* **Trigger:** The entity-intent summary is approved for analysis.  
* **Action:** Legal Counsel compares the available incorporation routes and records the trade-offs between speed/standardization and custom legal framing.  
* **Action:** Legal Counsel prepares a draft legal framing package, including proposed `object social`, activity classification assumptions, governance structure, and name strategy.  
* **Action:** Any founder-critical trade-off is documented explicitly, including what is gained or lost by choosing a more standardized route.  
* **Output:** A written route recommendation with decision rationale and open questions.

4.3. Capitalization and Evidence Readiness

* **Trigger:** The proposed route is close to approval.  
* **Action:** Legal Counsel checks whether the planned capital mix, in-kind contributions, founder documentation, and registered-office posture are sufficient to proceed legally.  
* **Action:** Legal Counsel coordinates with Finance when capitalization, shareholder-loan posture, or reimbursement treatment may affect incorporation decisions.  
* **Action:** Dependencies for startup certification, pluriactivity treatment, and trademark timing are logged as linked follow-on actions.  
* **Output:** A pre-signature readiness pack with legal dependencies clearly identified.

4.4. Pre-Signature Legal Review

* **Trigger:** Signature preparation begins.  
* **Action:** Legal Counsel verifies name options, shareholder/administrator structure, address posture, identity pack completeness, and any required bank/notary sequence.  
* **Action:** The founder receives a written summary of what will be fixed by signature and what remains changeable later at additional cost or complexity.  
* **Output:** A final legal-readiness confirmation or a blocked-state list with remediation actions.

4.5. Post-Incorporation Handoff

* **Trigger:** The deed is signed or the entity becomes operational.  
* **Action:** Legal Counsel issues a concise handoff note covering what was incorporated, which assumptions were used, and which linked tracks now activate.  
* **Action:** Handoffs are made to Finance for operating-funds governance, to Compliance for startup-certification readiness, and to Legal/Brand for trademark work when appropriate.  
* **Output:** A documented post-incorporation handoff package.

5.0 Roles and Responsibilities

* **Legal Counsel:** Owns the process, route recommendation, draft legal framing, and pre-signature decision log.  
* **Founder / Entity Sponsor:** Supplies intent, evidence, decisions, and approvals in time for incorporation sequencing.  
* **Business Controller / Taxes:** Advises where capitalization or founder-funding treatment affects legal setup.  
* **Compliance:** Receives startup-readiness dependencies that must be tracked after incorporation.  
* **Brand Manager:** Receives brand/naming dependencies where the filing path depends on brand architecture.

6.0 Addendum

A.1: Required Input Pack

* Founder intent statement  
* Proposed entity names in priority order  
* Draft service and revenue model summary  
* Shareholder/administrator structure  
* Registered-office proposal  
* Capitalization plan, including in-kind assets if applicable  
* Known future-structure constraints (holding, spinout, co-venture, investor entry)  
* Startup-certification ambition and timing assumption

A.2: Minimum Decision Questions

* What is the primary legal story of entity 1?  
* Which route is being recommended, and why?  
* What exact legal framing is being locked in at signature?  
* What assumptions still require accountant, notary, or startup-certification confirmation?  
* Which downstream tracks activate immediately after incorporation?

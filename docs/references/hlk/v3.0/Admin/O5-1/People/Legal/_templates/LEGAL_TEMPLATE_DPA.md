## 3. Processor obligations (GDPR Art. 28(3))

3.1. **Processing only on Controller instructions.** Processor processes Personal Data only on documented instructions from Controller, including with regard to transfers to third countries, unless required to do so by Union or Member-State law (in which case Processor informs Controller before processing, unless prohibited by law on important grounds of public interest).

3.2. **Confidentiality.** Processor ensures persons authorised to process Personal Data have committed to confidentiality or are under appropriate statutory obligation of confidentiality.

3.3. **Security measures (GDPR Art. 32).** Processor implements appropriate technical and organisational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk, including: pseudonymisation; encryption in transit and at rest where applicable; ability to ensure ongoing confidentiality, integrity, availability, and resilience of processing systems; ability to restore access in a timely manner after incident; regular testing of effectiveness of measures.

  **Annex II (Technical and Organisational Measures)** lists the specific measures Processor implements.

3.4. **Sub-processors.** Processor may engage sub-processors (a) with Controller's prior general written authorisation and a 30-day objection window for any change to the sub-processor list, or (b) on a case-by-case basis with prior specific written authorisation. Processor remains liable to Controller for sub-processor performance and imposes equivalent data-protection obligations on each sub-processor by written contract. **Annex III** lists currently-engaged sub-processors at Effective Date.

3.5. **Data-subject rights.** Processor assists Controller, by appropriate technical and organisational measures and to the extent reasonably possible, in fulfilling Controller's obligation to respond to data-subject requests under GDPR Chapter III.

3.6. **Personal data breaches.** Processor notifies Controller without undue delay (and in any event within 48 hours) of becoming aware of a personal data breach. Notification includes: nature of breach; categories and approximate number of data subjects + records concerned; likely consequences; measures taken or proposed.

3.7. **Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIA).** Processor reasonably assists Controller with DPIAs and prior consultations with supervisory authorities under GDPR Articles 35-36.

3.8. **Return or deletion at end of services.** At the choice of Controller, Processor deletes or returns all Personal Data to Controller after the end of the provision of Services, and deletes existing copies unless Union or Member-State law requires retention.

3.9. **Audit.** Processor makes available to Controller all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA. Processor allows for and contributes to audits, including inspections, conducted by Controller or another auditor mandated by Controller, no more than once per calendar year unless a personal data breach has occurred. Costs of audit are borne by Controller unless an audit reveals material non-compliance by Processor, in which case Processor bears reasonable audit costs.

## 4. Controller obligations

4.1. Controller is responsible for the lawfulness of the processing it instructs Processor to perform, including the legal basis for processing under GDPR Article 6 (and Article 9 for special-category data).

4.2. Controller provides Processor with documented instructions sufficient for Processor to comply with this DPA and the GDPR.

4.3. Controller is responsible for fulfilling its obligations as Controller under the GDPR, including providing privacy notices, obtaining consent where required, and responding to data-subject requests.

## 5. International transfers

5.1. Processor does not transfer Personal Data outside the EEA except: (a) to a country covered by a European Commission adequacy decision; (b) under Standard Contractual Clauses (Commission Decision 2021/914); or (c) under another lawful transfer mechanism documented in writing.

5.2. Sub-processor transfers outside the EEA require equivalent safeguards documented at the time of sub-processor engagement.

## 6. Liability

Liability under this DPA is governed by MSA Section 9 (Limitation of Liability), with the carve-out that liability under this DPA is not capped where caps would be inconsistent with mandatory data-protection law.

## 7. Term and termination

7.1. This DPA enters into force on the Effective Date and remains in force for the duration of the MSA and any active SOW that involves processing of Personal Data.

7.2. Sections 3.6 (breach notification), 3.8 (return/deletion), 3.9 (audit), and 6 (liability) survive termination to the extent applicable.

## 8. General provisions

Per MSA Section 11 (Governing law, Notices, Severability, Force majeure, Counterparts).

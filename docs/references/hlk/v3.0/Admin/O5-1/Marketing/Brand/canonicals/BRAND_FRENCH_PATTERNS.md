# BRAND_FRENCH_PATTERNS — French brand voice canonical

> **Status — Active (Initiative 66 P1; promoted from stub 2026-05-08).** Substantially expanded from the 2026-04-30 stub on the strength of two trigger inputs: (a) decision [D-IH-66-D](../../../../wip/planning/66-brand-vision-ops-sweep/decision-log.md#d-ih-66-d) to build deep voice canonicals in EN + ES + FR alongside the public-surface EN/ES/FR i18n parity (I66 P5), (b) operator-supplied transcripts of real FR exchanges (the `Rushly_Cahier_des_charges_v2.docx.md` deliverable + the `2026-04-01 - presentation projet 2.mp3.md` voice transcript) that anchored register decisions. Hand-authored companion to [`BRAND_VOICE_FOUNDATION.md`](BRAND_VOICE_FOUNDATION.md). Cited by the composer when `language_preference: fr` resolves at Layer 4.

## 1. Why this canonical exists

The brand voice charter and archetype in [`BRAND_VOICE_FOUNDATION.md`](BRAND_VOICE_FOUNDATION.md) are language-agnostic. French requires its own canonical because:

- **Vous/tu register is sharper than Spanish usted/tú.** French peers in B2B contexts maintain `vous` for far longer than Spanish-speaking peers maintain `usted`. Switching to `tu` too early reads as familiarity-claim and breaks rapport.
- **Anglicisms are a brand-identity signal.** French B2B audiences (especially in regulated industries — finance, energy, public sector) read anglicisms as "American startup imitation". Swapping `de-risker` for `réduire le risque` is a brand decision, not an aesthetic one.
- **Founder-name + entity-name dual presentation.** The founder's first name (`Fayçal`) is unmistakably French/Arabic-bilingual; the brand "Holistika" presents to French speakers as Greek-derived. The pairing creates a specific perceived identity ("research-grade, multicultural, technical") that the prose must reinforce, not undercut.
- **FR is the second-largest EU market after DE.** FR-language deliverables emerge as a near-term commercial reality (operator-noted in 2026-04 review).

## 2. Core register matrix

| Recipient context | Address form | Salutation | Closing | Notes |
|:---|:---:|:---|:---|:---|
| First contact, business prospect | `vous` | `Bonjour [Prénom],` (or `Madame, / Monsieur,` if last name only known) | `Cordialement,` | Default for unknown contacts |
| First contact, advisor / counsel / regulator | `vous` | `Bonjour [Prénom Nom],` (full name) | `Bien à vous,` (slightly warmer than `Cordialement`) | Used for ENISA-equivalent + financial counsel + legal counsel |
| Ongoing peer rapport (advisor or partner) | `vous` (default; `tu` only after they initiate) | `Bonjour [Prénom],` | `Cordialement,` then `Bien cordialement,` after 2-3 exchanges | The shift from `Cordialement` to `Bien cordialement` is a peer-rapport signal |
| Internal Holistika team (FR speakers) | `tu` (acceptable internally) | `Salut [Prénom],` | `À bientôt,` | Internal-only; never carry over to external |
| Customer (SME, B2B context) | `vous` | `Bonjour [Prénom],` | `Cordialement,` | Stay formal even after multiple exchanges; SMEs in FR expect this |
| Press / journalist | `vous` (always) | `Bonjour [Prénom Nom],` | `Cordialement,` | Always formal; press writes about brands they perceive as professional |
| LinkedIn DM (warm prospect) | `vous` | `Bonjour [Prénom],` | `Cordialement,` then `À bientôt,` only after meeting | DM register matches first-contact email register |

The composer's [`BRAND_REGISTER_MATRIX.md`](BRAND_REGISTER_MATRIX.md) lookup picks the **register**; this matrix picks the **opener / closer phrasing** within that register when `language_preference: fr`.

## 3. Reference exchange — `2026-04-01 presentation projet 2` (Founder ↔ FR prospect)

Operator-supplied voice transcript anchors the register decisions below. PII redacted per [`SOP-HLK_TRANSCRIPT_REDACTION_001.md`](../../People/Compliance/SOP-HLK_TRANSCRIPT_REDACTION_001.md).

### 3.1 Founder presentation opener (formal, vous)

> Bonjour, je suis Fayçal Njoya, fondateur de Holistika. Nous accompagnons des PME comme la vôtre sur des projets stratégiques où la question de la donnée et de la décision se croise.

**Pattern signals:**

- Self-introduction with **first name + last name + role + company** in the first sentence.
- Verb choice: `accompagner` (not `aider`, which reads as charity-helper; not `consulter`, which sounds salesy).
- `PME comme la vôtre` — adopting the prospect's frame ("we work with companies like yours") without overclaiming sector expertise.
- `où la question de la donnée et de la décision se croise` — abstracting the offering into a **frame** (data + decision) rather than a **deliverable list** (more in §4).

### 3.2 Founder transition to substance

> Concrètement, nous travaillons sur trois axes :
>
> - Un — la cartographie de votre opération actuelle ;
> - Deux — l'identification des points où l'information manque ou retarde la décision ;
> - Trois — la construction de processus mesurables qui réduisent ce retard.

**Pattern signals:**

- `Concrètement,` as a transition word — French B2B audiences expect a move from frame to substance, signaled with `Concrètement` or `En pratique`.
- Triadic structure (`trois axes`) — French rhetorical preference for triads (especially in pitch contexts).
- `Un — / Deux — / Trois —` numbering with em-dashes — formal but readable.
- `réduisent ce retard` — outcome-oriented; not `améliorer la performance` (vague).

### 3.3 Founder closer with concrete next step

> Si l'approche vous parle, je vous propose un échange de trente minutes la semaine prochaine — mardi ou jeudi en début d'après-midi. Je vous envoie un lien Calendly à la suite.

**Pattern signals:**

- `Si l'approche vous parle,` — softens the close; respects French B2B preference for non-pushy commerce.
- **Concrete time windows** (`mardi ou jeudi en début d'après-midi`) — avoids the vague `quand vous voulez`.
- `Je vous envoie un lien Calendly à la suite` — operator-explicit next step (matches Spanish pattern of stating concrete availability).

## 4. Patterns to follow

| When... | Holistika does | Holistika does NOT |
|:---|:---|:---|
| First-contact email | `Bonjour [Prénom], Je vous remercie pour votre message.` | `Salut, merci pour votre mail` (too informal) |
| Statement of capability | `Nous accompagnons des PME comme la vôtre sur...` | `Nous offrons des services de consulting...` (sales-template) |
| Acknowledging a referral | `[Référent] m'a évoqué votre démarche, et j'imagine que...` | `[Référent] m'a parlé de vous` (too casual) |
| Stating availability | `mardi ou jeudi en début d'après-midi` (concrete windows) | `quand vous voulez` or `selon votre convenance` (vague) |
| Confirming a meeting slot | `C'est noté pour mardi à 14h00.` | `Parfait, on se voit mardi !` (over-warm for first contact) |
| Closing a message after substance | `Cordialement,` (default) → `Bien à vous,` (slightly warmer) → `Bien cordialement,` (peer-warm after 3+ exchanges) | `À bientôt !` (over-warm for early relationship); `Sincères salutations` (over-formal for ongoing) |
| Stating uncertainty | `Je précise après vérification` or `Je reviens vers vous une fois l'analyse faite` | `Je crois que...` (uncommitted; reads weak in B2B FR) |
| Politely declining | `Ce n'est pas notre cœur de métier — je vous oriente vers [Prénom Nom] qui...` | `Désolé, on ne peut pas` (closes door without redirect) |
| Acknowledging mistake | `Effectivement, je me suis trompé sur ce point. Voici la correction :` | Vague apology + no correction |

## 5. Patterns to refuse — anglicisms, performative French, and corporate filler

Anchored in `voice_is_not` (Performative / Vague / Jargon-heavy) per [`BRAND_DO_DONT.md`](BRAND_DO_DONT.md):

### 5.1 Anglicisms to reject (specific FR challenge)

| Anglicism (avoid) | French replacement |
|:---|:---|
| `de-risquer` | `réduire le risque` |
| `mappinger` (the operation) | `cartographier` |
| `process` (singular) | `processus` |
| `pipeline` (in business sense) | `chaîne de valeur` or `enchaînement` (depending on context) |
| `framework` | `cadre méthodologique` or `cadre` |
| `deliverable` | `livrable` |
| `meeting` | `réunion` (or `point` for short calls) |
| `team building` | `animation d'équipe` or `cohésion d'équipe` |
| `feedback` (in formal contexts) | `retour` or `commentaires` |
| `roadmap` | `feuille de route` |
| `stakeholder` | `partie prenante` |
| `engagement` (in client-relationship sense) | `mission` (consulting) or `intervention` |
| `business case` | `cas d'affaire` or `argumentaire commercial` |
| `consulting` (the service) | `conseil` |
| `to scale` (a business) | `industrialiser` (process) or `passer à l'échelle` (operation) |
| `actionable` | `opérationnel` or `concret` |
| `playbook` | `manuel opérationnel` |
| `learnings` | `enseignements` |
| `benchmark` | `étalonnage` (technical) or `comparaison` |
| `core business` | `cœur de métier` |

This list is **not exhaustive**. The principle: if a French word exists, use it. Anglicisms in technical fields (computer science, finance) where the English term is the global standard (e.g., `API`, `cloud`, `SaaS`) are acceptable; anglicisms that have native French replacements are not.

### 5.2 Performative French to reject

- **Performative humility:** `Je serais honoré de pouvoir collaborer avec vous` → use `Avec plaisir de collaborer.` (warm but not performative).
- **Performative gratitude:** `Je vous remercie infiniment pour le temps que vous m'avez consacré et pour votre attention bienveillante` → use `Merci pour le temps consacré.`
- **Performative deference:** `Je me permets de me tourner vers vous concernant...` → use `Je vous écris au sujet de...`
- **Vague timeframes:** `Je vous reviens dans les meilleurs délais` → state when (`Je vous reviens vendredi avant 18h`).

### 5.3 Empty corporate filler to refuse

- `Dans le cadre de nos échanges...` → just say what's true (`Suite à votre message...`).
- `Étant entendu que...` → drop the windup; lead with the substance.
- `Pour faire court...` (then proceeds to write a long paragraph) → just be short.
- `Les enjeux sont multiples et la complexité est telle que...` → empty academic prelude; state the enjeu directly.

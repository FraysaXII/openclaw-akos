# Marketing Lifecycle Taxonomy

## 1. Purpose

This canonical defines the words Holistika uses from first market signal to offboarding. It resolves the operator's question: funnel, lead, engagement, customer, order-to-cash, and offboard are related but not the same thing.

The taxonomy is grounded in Wave R+4 Research Action sources `SRC-WR4-B-01` through `SRC-WR4-B-05` and maps industry Demand Waterfall / B2B Revenue Waterfall patterns into Holistika's operating model.

## 2. The 8-Stage Demand-to-Cash Scaffold

| Stage | Name | Plain meaning | Typical owner | Exit condition |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| 1 | Demand-Target | We know this audience or account class may matter. | Research + Reach | Target is named with source-backed rationale. |
| 2 | Demand-Active | We have a live outreach or inbound route. | Reach | Contact or channel interaction exists. |
| 3 | Demand-Engaged | The person or group has shown meaningful interest. | Reach + Resonance | Qualification question answered or meeting accepted. |
| 4 | Conversation-Live | There is a live commercial or strategic conversation. | Resonance + PMO | Need, timing, stakeholders, and next step are known. |
| 5 | Proposal-Active | A scoped offer or program note exists. | PMO + Storytelling/Brand | Proposal or brief sent with scope and decision path. |
| 6 | Engagement-Active | Work is signed or formally authorized. | PMO + delivery owner | PO, contract, or explicit written authorization exists. |
| 7 | Engagement-Retention | Delivery has ongoing renewal, expansion, or continuity surface. | Account Management / Resonance | Renewal or continuity path accepted or declined. |
| 8 | Engagement-Offboard | Relationship exits or pauses. | PMO + Account Management | Exit reason, artifacts, and follow-up posture recorded. |

## 3. Vocabulary Boundaries

| Term | Use it when | Do not use it when |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Funnel | Describing conversion movement across stages 1-5. | Referring to a signed delivery relationship. |
| Lead | Referring to a person/account before stage 4 is fully qualified. | Referring to an active signed customer. |
| Live conversation | Referring to stages 3-5 where interest and next steps exist but no PO/contract is signed. | Claiming traction as signed engagement. |
| Engagement | Referring to stage 6 onward: signed or formally authorized work. | Referring to SUEZ/Websitz as of Wave R+4 unless a PO/contract exists. |
| Customer | Referring to a paying or formally authorized counterparty. | Referring to an interested investor, partner, or lead. |
| Order-to-cash | Referring to the operational finance lifecycle after authorization. | Referring to pre-sales funnel motion. |
| Offboard | Referring to orderly exit, pause, or decline. | Hiding a decline or failed fit. |

## 4. Current Pipeline Honesty Rule

SUEZ and Websitz are **live commercial conversations** unless and until a PO, contract, invoice, or written authorization exists. They are not signed engagements and must not be described as customers.

This rule is mandatory for C4 investor outputs. The honest phrasing is:

> Holistika has a checked-in methodology stack, KiRBe + MADEIRA in active development, and live commercial conversations including SUEZ and Websitz.

## 5. Cross-Area Consumption

- **Research** identifies demand targets and source-backed topics.
- **Reach** opens and measures channel paths.
- **Resonance** deepens qualified conversations.
- **Brand & Narrative** authors the artifacts that move between stages.
- **PMO / RevOps** governs Proposal-Active through Engagement-Offboard.
- **Finance / Order-to-cash** begins at signed authorization, not before.

## 6. Verification

Before external prose claims pipeline traction, check:

1. Which stage is the counterparty actually in?
2. Which artifact proves it?
3. Which source ledger or CRM/ERP record supports it?
4. Is the wording external-translated and honest about unsigned state?

If any answer is missing, do not upgrade the stage in prose.

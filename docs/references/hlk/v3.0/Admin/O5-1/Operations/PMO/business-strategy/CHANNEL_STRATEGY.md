# Channel strategy — five hypotheses, each with a leading indicator

## What this answers

How Holística acquires customers, channel-by-channel, with each channel framed as a **testable hypothesis** rather than a generic "we'll do marketing" claim. Per 2026 GTM research: only 7 % of founders have a strong GTM narrative; the differentiator is naming a channel + its specific tactic + its CAC band + its current pilot evidence.

## Channel format (every entry below)

Each channel is a hypothesis card with: **Hypothesis** (the bet), **ICP × channel match** (who this channel reaches), **Leading indicator** (the metric we watch monthly), **Current evidence** (real conversations / engagements / signals), **CAC band** (estimated customer acquisition cost), **Decision criteria** (what tells us to scale, pause, or kill).

---

## Channel 1 — Founder-led direct outbound

**Hypothesis.** A weekly cadence of founder-led direct outreach (LinkedIn, warm email, Spanish startup community) to tech-enabled SMEs (10 - 80 employees) closes 1 - 2 paid Strategic Audits per month at €4 - 8 k each.

**ICP × channel match.** Pyme tecnológica with founder-CEO who reads LinkedIn; Spanish-language conversations preferred; engagement gates on the founder's network credibility, not on cold-email volume.

**Leading indicator.** Weekly: 5 - 10 first conversations initiated. Monthly: 1 - 2 audit engagements signed.

**Current evidence.** TODO[OPERATOR-channel1-evidence] (band: founder has ~X warm contacts in Spanish startup network; ~Y % have raised a "we should be more organised" complaint in the last 6 months).

**CAC band.** €100 - €500 per audit (mostly founder time + LinkedIn premium + travel). Margin per audit ~75 %.

**Decision criteria.** Scale if conversion rate ≥ 10 % first-conversation-to-audit. Pause if < 5 %. Kill if founder time-cost exceeds 30 % of weekly capacity for 4 + weeks.

**Why this channel works first.** Founder-led sales is the proven 2026 SaaS pattern under €1 M ARR; deal size €5 - 50 k ACV is the founder-led sweet spot; no need for a sales hire yet.

---

## Channel 2 — Partner B2B (Websitz pattern)

**Hypothesis.** Two structured partnerships with EU-based product agencies (Spanish and one EU neighbour) generate 1 - 2 sub-contracted delivery engagements per quarter, each €15 - 40 k, with Holística delivering software + method.

**ICP × channel match.** Partner B2B clients (integrators, product agencies) who have client capacity but lack engineering depth. Holística is the engineering-+-method layer; partner owns the relationship.

**Leading indicator.** Monthly: number of partner-introduced opportunities; quarterly: signed sub-contracted engagements.

**Current evidence.** Holística × Websitz delivered the Shopify cart-bundle app (one closed engagement). Rushly partnership at scaffold stage. **Two confirmed-active relationships.**

**CAC band.** Near-zero per engagement (partner runs the relationship; Holística pays standard partner share — see `PRICING_MODEL.md` §1.3).

**Decision criteria.** Scale if 2 + closed engagements per quarter for 2 consecutive quarters. Add a third partner if so. Restructure share if margin compresses below 30 %.

**Why this channel matters.** Recurring engagements, low CAC, channel-effect compounds (each partner → more partners → eventually a partner-of-partners network). In 2026 SaaS, partner channels typically contribute 20 - 35 % of total revenue when active.

---

## Channel 3 — KiRBe inbound (content + product-led)

**Hypothesis.** The KiRBe public site (with a free trial, content marketing in Spanish + English, and SEO around "governed knowledge management") generates a steady inbound stream — initially 3 - 5 trial signups per week, converting at 5 - 10 % to paid Starter / Team tier.

**ICP × channel match.** Cliente SaaS (technical teams adopting KiRBe directly) who find us via search or content. English-content-driven for international reach; Spanish-language content for local credibility.

**Leading indicator.** Weekly: trial signups. Monthly: trial-to-paid conversion rate. Quarterly: monthly recurring revenue (MRR) growth.

**Current evidence.** TODO[OPERATOR-channel3-evidence] (band: KiRBe live for internal use; public site presence currently TODO; first 5 paying customers TODO).

**CAC band.** €50 - €300 per paid customer at scale (content + ads + onboarding cost). Per 2026 PLG benchmarks, < €5 k ACV with 3 - 6 month CAC payback.

**Decision criteria.** Scale paid acquisition if organic conversion ≥ 5 %. Tighten content if < 2 %. Kill paid if CAC > €500.

**Why this channel matters.** Recurring SaaS revenue; product-led growth scales without proportional founder-time; this is the long-term engine.

---

## Channel 4 — Inbound from Holística public site

**Hypothesis.** A modest content / SEO / case-study presence on holistikaresearch.com generates 1 - 2 audit-shaped enquiries per month with a 30 - 50 % audit-conversion rate.

**ICP × channel match.** Pyme tecnológica found via search ("how to structure operations before scaling", "Spain SME AI strategy", "consultoría de procesos para startups").

**Leading indicator.** Monthly: enquiry volume; quarterly: signed audit count from inbound.

**Current evidence.** Public site live (multilingual). Currently configured for marketing, not lead-magnet driven (no gated content, no calculator, no audit-prep checklist).

**CAC band.** €0 - €200 per audit (content production cost amortised over many enquiries).

**Decision criteria.** Invest in 3 - 5 case studies + 1 lead magnet if Channel 1 is bottlenecked by founder-time.

**Why this channel matters.** Compound effect: every closed engagement becomes a case study; every case study lifts inbound; this is the bootstrap path.

---

## Channel 5 — Events / community

**Hypothesis.** Speaking at 2 - 3 Spanish-startup-ecosystem events per year (4YFN, South Summit, Foundation events, ecosystem meetups) lifts brand recognition by ~10 % and produces 0.5 - 1 high-quality engagement per event.

**ICP × channel match.** Reaches Pyme tecnológica founders + investors + future partners simultaneously. Spanish-language preferred.

**Leading indicator.** Per event: number of speaking slots booked, number of attendees engaged, number of follow-up conversations.

**Current evidence.** TODO[OPERATOR-channel5-evidence] (operator history with the Spanish startup ecosystem, prior speaking slots, etc.).

**CAC band.** €1 000 - €3 000 per event (travel + prep time). Spreads across multiple engagements.

**Decision criteria.** Continue if at least 1 closed engagement traceable per event. Pivot to investor-only events if raising. Kill if < 1 closed for 2 consecutive events.

**Why this channel matters.** Slow but high-trust; complements Channel 1 founder-led outbound; trains the founder's narrative repeatedly.

## Hypotheses to retire

- "Paid Google Ads will scale fast" — explicitly rejected. SME B2B with €5 - 40 k ACV doesn't pay back paid search reliably until KiRBe SaaS hits scale.
- "Cold outbound at high volume" — rejected. Founder-led at low volume + high quality wins; cold-mass-mail damages brand.
- "Conferences as primary channel" — rejected. Too slow / too expensive as a primary; remains opportunistic.

# Market thesis — what we believe about the market

## What this answers

Why now, what shift is structural, who's already serving the market, and where the white space is.

## 1. The structural shift we're betting on

Companies are adopting AI tools faster than they can operationalise them. The bottleneck isn't access to models — it's the messy, undocumented business knowledge that AI needs to do useful work. The companies that win the next 5 years aren't the ones with more tools; they're the ones whose operations are ordered enough that any tool — including AI — produces measurable results.

Holística sits at that bottleneck. We turn messy operations into systems that can be executed, automated, audited, and improved.

## 2. Why now (three forces compounding)

**Force 1 — AI-driven operational pressure.** Every SME is asked "what's your AI strategy?" by their board, by their customers, and by their team. Most have no operational substrate to plug AI into; they buy tools, struggle, blame the tools. The 2026 default response is "we need to ordenar el flujo before we layer AI on top".

**Force 2 — Compliance and audit pressure.** EU AI Act, GDPR enforcement maturity, sector-specific regulations (finance, health, education) all push toward auditable systems. A company that can't show *why* a decision was made loses the contract.

**Force 3 — Lean teams + remote distribution.** Post-2024, SMEs are leaner. They can't hire a full-time consulting firm; they need a partner who shows up, designs the system, deploys software, and disappears with documentation. That's a productized service shape, not classic consulting.

## 4. Existing market structure

Three player categories serve adjacent problems today:

- **Classic consulting** — McKinsey, BCG, Big Four; high cost, slow, project-based; rarely ship software.
- **Boutique product agencies** — Spanish + EU agencies; ship software but rarely come with method or operations rigour.
- **Pure SaaS** — Notion, Asana, Monday, etc.; sell tools but require the customer to have already-ordered operations.

Holística occupies the **diagonal** — method + software + governed delivery — that none of the three serve well.

## 5. Anti-thesis (what we don't believe)

- We don't believe "AI will figure it out" — the messy-operations problem is upstream of the model.
- We don't believe consulting needs to take 6 months — productized + software-shipped engagements close in weeks.
- We don't believe SMEs want a tool that requires a full-time admin — they want the operating system delivered, not curated.

## 6. Risks to the thesis

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|:---|:---|:---|
| AI tooling matures fast enough that the messy-operations problem is solved by the tools themselves | Medium | We bet that the operational substrate (governance, audit trails, knowledge structure) is itself the moat — tools assume it; they don't create it |
| EU regulatory pressure plateaus | Low | Ample evidence of continued regulatory tightening (AI Act 2024 - 2026 phased rollout) |
| Classic consulting moves into productized delivery | High | Their cost structure prevents serving the SME band; we don't compete with them on enterprise |
| Pure SaaS adds methodology layers | Medium | Tools can't replace the bespoke audit + design phase; we lead with that |

## 7. What this thesis demands of us (next 24 months)

- Ship 5 - 10 paying client engagements end-to-end (cement the method's deliverability).
- Get KiRBe to 5 - 10 paying B2B clients (demonstrate productisation).
- Build a partner channel with 2 - 3 integrators (proves the channel ROI).

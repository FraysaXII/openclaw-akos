# Sequence Templates

> External-register outreach and follow-up templates. Each sequence is deliberately short: first message, value follow-up, evidence follow-up, close/permission message. Do not automate these blindly; use them as controlled starting points.

## Investor Sequence

### 1. First Message

Subject: Holistika Research, operating systems for companies

Hi [Name],

I am reaching out because Holistika sits at the intersection of research, execution, and internal software. We help companies make their operations legible before they scale, then productize the repeatable parts through our technology lab.

The short proof: prior company-building experience, an active product stack, and a service model that validates the software before it is sold as software.

Would a 25-minute introduction be useful next week?

Faycal

### 2. Evidence Follow-Up

Hi [Name],

Adding the one-page version so you can decide quickly:

- Holistika Research SL is the legal entity.
- Holistika is the umbrella brand.
- Holistika R&S, Think Big, and HLK Tech Lab are delivery modes under one company.
- The company is service-led today, with a software path already built through the lab.

If this is relevant, I can send the investor deck before a short call.

### 3. Close / Permission

Hi [Name],

I will close the loop here unless this is relevant for your current thesis. If it is, I can send the deck and suggest three times.

## Customer-SME Sequence

### 1. First Message

Subject: Making your operations easier to run

Hi [Name],

Holistika helps companies make their operations clearer, more repeatable, and easier to hand over to a team.

We usually start with a short discovery call: what is working, what is stuck in people's heads, what repeats every month, and what would need to be true for the company to scale with less friction.

If useful, I can suggest a simple agenda for a first conversation.

### 2. Value Follow-Up

Hi [Name],

The work is not a generic transformation package. We map the operating reality first, then decide whether the right output is a process redesign, a business-engineering brief, a technical integration, or a capability-building program.

The goal is a useful system, not a deck that sits in a folder.

### 3. Close / Permission

Hi [Name],

I will leave this here for now. If the timing becomes relevant, the right next step is a 30-minute discovery conversation with one concrete operating problem on the table.

## Advisor Sequence

### 1. First Message

Subject: A precise advisory ask

Hi [Name],

I would value your advice on one specific question: [question].

Context in two lines: Holistika Research SL combines research, execution, and a technology lab. We are currently deciding [decision], and your perspective would help us avoid a weak assumption.

Would you be open to a 20-minute call?

### 2. Preparation Follow-Up

Hi [Name],

To make this useful, I will send a short brief before the call:

- the decision we need to make,
- what we already know,
- what remains uncertain,
- what I am specifically asking you to challenge.

### 3. Close / Permission

Hi [Name],

No pressure if this is not a good fit. I will only follow up if there is a precise ask where your input would be genuinely useful.

## Partner Sequence

### 1. First Message

Subject: Possible co-delivery fit

Hi [Name],

I am exploring whether Holistika could complement your work rather than compete with it.

We are strongest where a partner has commercial access, domain depth, or implementation capacity, and needs structured research, operating-model design, or technical delivery around it.

Would it be useful to compare scopes for 30 minutes?

### 2. Fit Follow-Up

Hi [Name],

The useful question is not "can we partner?" It is: where would the client be better served by both teams than by either team alone?

If we cannot answer that clearly, we should not force the partnership.

### 3. Close / Permission

Hi [Name],

I will close this thread unless you see a concrete overlap worth testing. A small pilot is better than a broad partnership statement.

## Recruiter Sequence

Holistika is building a company around research, execution, and technology. The work suits people who can handle ambiguity but want strong structure: clear writing, explicit decisions, reusable systems, and real ownership.

I would like to discuss whether [role / profile] fits what we are building.

### 2. Evidence Follow-Up

Hi [Name],

The signal I would point to is not a culture slogan. It is the operating discipline: canon, SOPs, templates, drift checks, and a technology lab that builds internal tools before selling them externally.

If that sounds like the kind of environment [candidate / you] would respect, we should talk.

### 3. Close / Permission

Hi [Name],

No issue if the timing is wrong. I would rather keep the conversation precise than force a generic hiring thread.

## ENISA Sequence

### 1. First Message

Subject: Holistika Research SL, technological component and scalability evidence

Hi [Name],

Holistika Research SL is preparing the evidence package for a company model that combines service revenue with an internal technology lab.

The core question is whether the technological component, scalability path, employment plan, and capital-efficiency evidence are clear enough for review.

Would you be open to reviewing the evidence structure?

### 2. Evidence Follow-Up

Hi [Name],

The package separates four claims:

- the legal entity and brand architecture,
- the service-to-software delivery model,
- the internal product stack,
- the founder's anonymized operating track record.

That separation is intentional: each claim can be verified independently.

### 3. Close / Permission

Hi [Name],

I will not keep following up if this is outside your scope. If it is relevant, I can send the 8-slide evidence deck and the supporting checklist.

## Usage Rules

- Replace placeholders before sending.
- Match the recipient's language and register.
- Never send the same sequence to two audiences without rewriting the opening frame.
- Attach a deck only after the recipient has enough context to read it correctly.
- Log meaningful replies in the relevant engagement or advisor register.

# I-NN-RESEARCH-OPS-SUBSTRATE — 10-pillar Holistika Research Operations frame

> **Spawned by D-IH-86-CW** (operator META1-a ratification 2026-05-24 at I86 Wave R+1 OPS-86-22 + OPS-86-23 attack gate). Operator's META1-a verbatim selection: *"m1-a-extend-8pillar"* — extend Boulton's industry-standard 8-pillar Research Operations frame with Holistika-specific pillars 9 (Brand) + 10 (UX) so the resulting 10-pillar frame becomes the canonical lens through which Research-area Ops gets operationalised inside Holistika. This is the **first deep worked example** for the 10-pillar Holistika ReOps frame; FINOPS is the second; the remaining 5 areas (Marketing / Tech Lab / Legal / Operations / People) get paragraph framings in I88.

This candidate is the **Research OPS-side mint** that complements the broader I88 Cross-Area Ops Wiring Review activation. Both candidates work together: I88 governs *how* areas wire their Ops surface across each other (the structural-integrity discipline); this candidate governs *what* the Research area's own Ops surface looks like (the content-shape discipline) — and how the 10-pillar frame travels horizontally to every other area as a shared lens.

## 1. The 10-pillar frame (one paragraph)

Industry consensus on Research Operations (per Kate Towsey + ReOps Community + NN/g + Boulton ReOps book) crystallised around 8 pillars: (1) Strategy & Planning, (2) Recruitment & Admin, (3) Tools & Infrastructure, (4) Knowledge Management, (5) Governance, Ethics, & Privacy, (6) Skills, Methods, & Capability, (7) Internal Communications & Advocacy, (8) Asks & Logistics. Holistika extends this baseline with two pillars that the operator's META1-a framing names load-bearing: **(9) Brand** (research outputs are brand-touching surfaces — every report carries the brand voice + dual-register vocabulary contract + render-trail per `akos-external-render-discipline.mdc`) and **(10) UX** (research is consumed via UX surfaces — dossiers, decks, dashboards, ERP panels — whose UX quality determines whether the research lands or doesn't). The 10 pillars form the canonical lens for **every area's** Ops surface, not just Research's — but Research is the first deep worked example because it carries the highest concentration of all 10 pillars in a single area.

## 2. Why the operator's META1-a framing is load-bearing

The pre-amendment Boulton 8 frame is sufficient to operationalise a UX-research function at scale (its origin context). It is **insufficient** to operationalise Holistika's Research area because:

1. **Holistika research outputs are brand-touching by default.** Every research deliverable (dossier, deck, report, recommendation) is rendered through a brand-register lens (per `akos-brand-baseline-reality.mdc` dual-register contract) and ships through a render-trail (per `akos-external-render-discipline.mdc` 6-surface enum). Brand is not a downstream consumer of research; **brand is a research-Ops pillar** that gates whether research outputs ship at all.
2. **UX of the research output determines whether it lands.** A research finding rendered through a dossier the customer never opens is worth less than zero (it consumed Ops bandwidth without delivering value). The UX of the dossier / deck / dashboard / ERP panel that carries the research is a Research-Ops concern, not just a UX-area concern. Per the forward-charter `UX_DISCIPLINE.md` (D-IH-86-AX; status: charter at Wave M P5), UX is recognised as a Quality Fabric specialty — and Research Ops needs UX as a pillar because Research is one of UX's primary consumer areas.
3. **The two new pillars cross-pollinate.** Brand and UX both inform pillar 4 (Knowledge Management — how research outputs are stored, indexed, retrieved, and presented) + pillar 7 (Internal Communications & Advocacy — how research findings travel inside the org). Adding them as standalone pillars makes their cross-pollination explicit + auditable.

## 4. Cross-pollination — the 10 pillars travel to every area

Per META2-b ratification, the 10-pillar frame is **not Research-specific**. It is a lens that every area's Ops surface can be examined through:

- **FINOPS** as second deep worked example (I88 P0 deliverable): the 10 pillars map onto FINOPS Ops surface (Strategy = finance roadmap; Recruitment = vendor onboarding; Tools = Stripe + Supabase + KiRBe billing plane + AT-Pymes gestoría; KM = FINOPS counterparty + engagement + filed-instruments register; Governance = RBAC + RLS + canonical-CSV gate; Skills = FINOPS bridge SOP + paired runbook; Comms = FINOPS dashboard + monthly close cadence; Asks = expense intake + per-vendor onboarding ask; Brand = finance-facing outbound brand voice; UX = FINOPS dashboard UX).
- **Marketing / Tech Lab / Legal / Operations / People** as paragraph framings (I88 P0 deliverable each): for each area, a short paragraph names how the 10 pillars currently land on that area's Ops surface + which pillars are most active / quietest / next to mature.

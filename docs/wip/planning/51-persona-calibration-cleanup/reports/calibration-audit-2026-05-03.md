# I51 / P2 — Calibration audit (R-47-2 13-outlier remediation plan)

This is the operator-facing calibration audit emitted at I51/P2. It captures
the baseline state of `PERSONA_SCENARIO_REGISTRY.csv` (post I50/P5
telemetry-promotion merges; **329** rows total, **326** `lifecycle_status=active`)
against the global D-IH-47-C calibration target
**40 / 40 / 10 / 10** with **±5pp** tolerance, identifies the 13 personas
outside tolerance, and proposes a per-persona remediation under the
**D-IH-51-A** default path (per-persona `target_difficulty_band` column,
not scenario rebalance).

The next phase (**I51/P3**) edits `PERSONA_SCENARIO_REGISTRY.csv` per this
plan under the **G-51-1** operator gate; this audit is the input the operator
ratifies before the CSV mutation lands.

## Method

- **Source artifact:** `artifacts/calibration/calibration-baseline-20260503T172852Z.json`
  (generated by `py scripts/calibrate_scenarios.py --quiet` on 2026-05-03 at
  17:28:52 UTC against repo HEAD `05c44ff` after the I51/P1 commit).
- **Inclusion rule:** `lifecycle_status=active` only (matches
  `akos.eval_harness.persona.calibration_distribution()` semantics; scaffold,
  deprecated, and quarantined rows are excluded). Active rows: 326.
- **Tolerance:** D-IH-47-C — a band is "within tolerance" iff
  `|delta_pp| ≤ 5.0`. A persona "passes" iff every band is within tolerance.
- **Drift sign convention:** `delta_pp = pct_actual − pct_target`.
  Positive = over-represented in that band; negative = under-represented.

## Proposed `target_difficulty_band` schema

The column is added under D-IH-51-A as part of **I51/P3**:

- **Column name:** `target_difficulty_band`
- **Position:** appended to the end of `PERSONA_SCENARIO_REGISTRY.csv` after
  `notes` (or before `notes` — choice deferred to I51/P3 commit; backward-
  compatibility wins either way because column is append-only).
- **Format:** `<trivial>/<moderate>/<hard>/<impossible>` integer percentages
  summing to **exactly 100**. Empty = "fall through to global D-IH-47-C
  40/40/10/10".
- **Validation:** `scripts/validate_persona_scenario_registry.py` learns a
  new check: per `persona_id`, all `target_difficulty_band` values must be
  identical (it's a persona-level attribute carried on every row of that
  persona for join-friendliness). The check returns
  `target_difficulty_band_per_persona_consistent: PASS|FAIL`.
- **Calibrator wiring:** `akos.eval_harness.persona.calibration_distribution`
  consults the per-persona band; falls through to the global default for
  personas with empty bands. The renderer surfaces the chosen target on the
  per-persona row of the calibration markdown (so the dossier doesn't lie).

## Exit criteria for I51/P3 (G-51-1)

The phase exits **GREEN** iff after the CSV edit + per-persona target
adoption:

1. `__overall__` remains within tolerance against the global default
   (40/40/10/10). The CSV edit doesn't move scenarios; only target bands;
   so this stays trivially PASS.
2. **≤ 2 personas** (out of 17) remain outside tolerance against
   **their own** `target_difficulty_band`. Per the proposals above, the
   two thinnest personas (CUSTOMER-SERVICE-PROSPECT and IDEA-PROPOSER, both
   n=6) carry the highest residual band-imbalance risk and may require an
   additional scenario tranche before they fully clear; they are explicitly
   the candidates for the "≤ 2 personas" allowance.
3. The new `validate_persona_scenario_registry.py` check passes (no
   inconsistent target bands within a persona).
4. `py scripts/calibrate_scenarios.py --quiet` reports
   **0 outlier personas** OR **exactly the 2 thin-N personas** above.
5. `py scripts/check-drift.py` is clean.
6. The `--filter madeira` dossier (next-cycle re-emit at I51/P6) shows the
   remediated calibration and the dossier's three lights stay GREEN.

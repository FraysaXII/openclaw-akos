# I67 Research Brief

## Research Question

What revenue-operations system should Holistika build next so brand, services, lab products, outreach, proposals, partner motions, onboarding, retention, and measurement reinforce one another?

## Required Inputs

- I66 outputs: brand canon, service catalog, P6 decks, sequences, proposal template, engagement playbook, and direct-access `/services` + `/how-we-work` decision.
- Operator interviews: founder ambition, current leads, current blockers, trusted channels, pricing discomforts, partner economics, and capacity constraints.
- Transcript mining: business developer onboarding, researcher onboarding, ENISA/company-formation calls, hospitality consulting calls, partner/customer calls, and WhatsApp fragments.
- External benchmarks: Stripe, Linear, Vercel, Notion, high-trust boutique consulting firms, productized-service firms, and EU-funding advisory providers.

## Method

1. Inventory sources.
2. Extract funnel events: trigger, audience, channel, objection, proof used, close/no-close signal.
3. Compare against external benchmarks.
4. Identify decisions that require operator approval.
5. Only then propose implementation phases.

## Expected Output

A scoped implementation plan with:

- audience journey map,
- channel strategy,
- offer packaging,
- pricing hypothesis,
- partner/referral motion,
- content/proof asset plan,
- tooling recommendation,
- KPI model,
- risk register.

## Non-Negotiables

- Research before roadmap.
- Evidence before taste.
- Operator decisions surfaced explicitly.
- I66 direct-access pages stay unlisted until I67 says otherwise and the operator approves.

# Research action — Infonomics / Holistika data economics

## Topic identity (not initiative-prefixed)

This pack is **topic-keyed** under the Research area Tier-1 WIP home (`docs/wip/intelligence/`) per the governed KB integrity move (**D-IH-95-H**). **Initiative 97** owns execution and vault tranches; the pack title stays on the **economic treatment of information and data as assets**.

## Problem statement (plain language)

Holistika already governs data quality, research trust, FINOPS registers, RevOps value-mapping, collaborator share economics, and three-plane research architecture — but **no single doctrine** names how information and data create, capture, and destroy economic value across areas and roles. Prior KB sweeps found **zero literal “Infonomics”** hits in the vault; the work is to **compose** fragmented adjacent doctrine into one governed narrative before any Tier-A canonical mint (scheduled **P6**, not dropped).

## Core research question

> How should Holistika treat information and data as **economic assets** — valuation, ownership, cost/benefit, quality ROI, incentive alignment, and cross-area handoffs — so that governed decisions (process_list, FINOPS, Research Action, area maturity, HCAM) cite a shared vocabulary?

## Research questions

1. What **internal CORPINT** already implies about data/information as assets (DAMA, FINOPS, RevOps spine, collaborator share, Research Action, three-plane architecture)?
2. What **external Infonomics canon** (Laney, DCAM, data mesh economics, FinOps/token economics) applies without collapsing into generic “data is the new oil” prose?
3. Which **economic levers** bind to each O5-1 area and baseline consumer prong (`BL-*`)?
4. Where do **skeptic / tradeoff** sources challenge Holistika’s implicit valuation posture (≥2 per load-bearing prong)?
5. How does **I96 Research Center** economics overlap this doctrine without duplicate Track D scope (ratify at P5)?

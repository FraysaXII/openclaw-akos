# Prong BL-PEOPLE — People / Quality Fabric consumer

> **Baseline prong:** `BL-PEOPLE` (O5-1 People / Quality Fabric)  
> **Ledger coverage:** 37 rows (15 CORPINT + 22 OSINT); **5 skeptic** voices  
> **Downstream decision:** **D-INF-ECON** (P5 govern)

## Narrative findings

### 1. Quality Fabric multiplies governance cost — and value — across every artifact

**Holistika Quality Fabric** (`SRC-INF-INT-114`) composes audience × channel × scenario × brand × governance — each quality-bound artifact pays a **five-axis tax** before ship. **UAT Discipline** (`SRC-INF-INT-120`) and paired SOPs (`SRC-INF-INT-118`, `SRC-INF-INT-117`) encode closure economics: World Quality Report 2024 (`SRC-INF-EXT-154`) cites **38% fewer post-release defects** and **52% fewer escalations** when UAT is documented — the ROI case for People-owned verification as information insurance.

Without pricing that tax, Infonomics understates the **carrying cost of trust**.

### 2. Area governance and maturity bar treat organizational capability as asset

**Area Governance Discipline** (`SRC-INF-INT-112`), **SOP-PEOPLE_AREA_GOVERNANCE_001** (`SRC-INF-INT-116`), and **Holistika Agentic Doctrine** (`SRC-INF-INT-113`) score areas on completeness — structurally similar to DCAM levels (`SRC-INF-EXT-004`, Data prong) but for **organizational information capability**. **Learning charter** (`SRC-INF-INT-169`) and onboarding curricula (`SRC-INF-INT-171`) invest in human capital that maintains register quality.

### 3. Collaborator economics skepticism warns about platform labor extraction

**Collaborator economics — platform labor critique** (`SRC-INF-EXT-492`, NBER working paper harvest) argues share-based models can **shift risk to collaborators** while capturing data surplus — directly relevant to **Collaborator Share Registry** (`SRC-INF-INT-004`, FIN prong). Holistika's share doctrine (`SRC-INF-INT-045`) must be evaluated as **incentive design**, not only spreadsheet accuracy.

### 4. UAT and testing skeptics push back on cargo-cult quality spend

**UAT cargo cult skeptic** (`SRC-INF-EXT-497`), agile-without-UAT (`SRC-INF-EXT-163`), and TestRail/UAT guide rows (`SRC-INF-EXT-158`, `SRC-INF-EXT-159`) challenge whether formal UAT pays off for every tranche. **Martin Fowler test pyramid** (`SRC-INF-EXT-089`) and **script sprawl postmortem** (`SRC-INF-EXT-090`) caution against automation inventory growth without economic pruning — ties to Tech verify profile COGS.

### 5. Second-system and accessibility limits bound "quality infinite spend"

**Second system effect** (`SRC-INF-EXT-319`) warns replatforming resets economic value; **accessibility audit limits** (`SRC-INF-EXT-166`) show compliance testing has **diminishing marginal assurance**. **ISO/IEC 25010** (`SRC-INF-EXT-155`) and **SonarQube quality gates** (`SRC-INF-EXT-156`) provide external quality models — Holistika validators mirror this but rarely attach **EUR per finding prevented**.

## Porter — four forces + competition synthesis

| Force | Finding (cite `SRC-*`) |
|:---|:---|
| **Supplier power** | Quality tool vendors (SonarQube `SRC-INF-EXT-156`, TestRail `SRC-INF-EXT-158`) and training content suppliers set price for assurance; collaborators (`SRC-INF-EXT-492`) supply labor under share terms (`SRC-INF-INT-004`). |
| **Buyer power** | Clients demand closure UAT (`SRC-INF-INT-120`); skeptics (`SRC-INF-EXT-497`) empower internal buyers (engineering) to skip UAT — economic tension inside the firm. |
| **Threat of substitutes** | Informal testing + "move fast" substitutes formal UAT (`SRC-INF-EXT-163`); generic LLM review substitutes structured synthesis before govern gate — faster, ungraded. |
| **Threat of new entrants** | Agencies without Quality Fabric ship faster at lower assurance cost — entrants compete until a defect triggers escalation (WQR `SRC-INF-EXT-154` downside). |
| **Competition synthesis** | Holistika sells **governed assurance** as economic product. People prong Infonomics must price Quality Fabric + UAT as **insurance premium** with skeptic-adjusted ROI (`SRC-INF-EXT-497`, `SRC-INF-EXT-089`), not infinite quality spend. |

## Infonomics hook

**Economic levers for People/Quality Fabric:** UAT/regression as defect-cost avoidance (WQR `SRC-INF-EXT-154`); area maturity as capability asset; collaborator share as labor incentive (`SRC-INF-EXT-492` risk-adjusted); validator suite as recurring COGS; learning investment as maintenance capex for register quality.

**Holistika delta vs external Infonomics posture:** industry buys **tools and certifications**; Holistika buys **multi-axis composition discipline** — higher fixed cost, higher differentiation if priced explicitly to clients and internal chargeback.

**Govern options (ranked):**

1. **Quality Fabric "assurance COGS" appendix in UNIT ECONOMICS** (`SRC-INF-INT-111`) — default hours per closure UAT + verify profile run. *(recommended)*
2. **ICS-weighted deferral policy** — WSJF (`SRC-INF-EXT-054`) on quality work; ties People cost to Ops prioritization.
3. **Amend COLLABORATOR_SHARE_DOCTRINE with economic fairness review** triggered by `SRC-INF-EXT-492` — FIN+People joint gate.
4. **Reduce Quality Fabric axes for internal-only artifacts** — saves cost, risks brand/compliance drift; only with operator ratify.

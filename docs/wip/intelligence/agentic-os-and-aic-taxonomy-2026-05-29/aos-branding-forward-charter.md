# Deferred: should Holistika claim the "AOS" label?

> The operator asked this and answered honestly: *"I can't say. As you said it's
> new, I don't know if everyone will understand. A deeper research must be made
> for branding."* This charter records the deferral cleanly so it is not lost,
> and scopes the reusable research that would answer it.

## Why deferred (not answered now)

The 2026-05-29 taxonomy sweep was scoped to **what the categories are**, not
**what to call ourselves**. Answering a positioning question from a taxonomy
source base would be the research-as-ornament anti-pattern. The branding question
needs its own source base: audience comprehension, competitor naming, trademark
scope, and register (internal vs external) per the dual-register brand discipline.

## The interim posture (from the taxonomy sweep, not a branding verdict)

- **Internal register:** AKOS may be described as a *KB-first / coordination-layer
  agentic operating system* — accurate per the taxonomy.
- **External register:** do **not** reflexively claim the bare "AOS" label — the
  field calls it "agent washing"; the term is diluted. AKOS's differentiated
  identity (*the operating system whose deliverables compound off a governed
  knowledge base, with governance + graded provenance on every action*) is
  stronger and more defensible than a crowded label.
- This is an *interim* posture for accuracy, **not** the branding decision.

## What the branding research would cover (when chartered)

1. **Audience comprehension** — do investors / customers / partners parse "agentic
   operating system" as meaningful or as buzzword? Per-audience (8 J-* codes).
2. **Competitive naming** — who already claims AOS / agent-OS / AIOS; is the label
   ownable or commoditized; the consolidation thesis (3-4 survivors).
3. **Trademark + legal scope** — is any AOS-class term registrable in Holistika's
   classes/jurisdictions.
4. **Register translation** — internal "KB-first AOS" → external phrasing per the
   brand baseline-reality matrix (dual register).
5. **The KB-compounding claim** — whether to lead positioning with the moat the
   field is weakest on (governance + graded provenance + compounding deliverables).

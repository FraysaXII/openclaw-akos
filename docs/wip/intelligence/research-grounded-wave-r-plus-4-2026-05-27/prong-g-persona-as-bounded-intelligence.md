# Prong G — Persona-as-bounded-intelligence (non-creepy POI/GOI industry practice)

## TL;DR for the C2 governance commit

The operator's 2026-05-27 framing named verbatim:

> *"the management of the intelligence about the persona, the persona as a POI/GOI too (not that much that will result creepy, just to research what we need to know is enough, so in that regard, i'm happy but i think we're not wiring what we have and that could escalate even with kirbe or deep research)."*

**Research finding (load-bearing):** The 2026 industry consensus across 4 sources surveyed converges on **6 GDPR-compliant principles** for non-creepy B2B persona intelligence that map directly to the existing Holistika `GOI_POI_STANCE_DOCTRINE.md` (active status) with **2 specific reinforcements** that should land at C2:

1. **Legal basis = legitimate interest (GDPR Article 6(1)(f))**, not consent. Requires documented Legitimate Interest Assessment (LIA) per campaign. [Derrick / Instantly / Unify]
2. **Three conditions cumulatively required**: (a) outreach is RELEVANT to the prospect's professional role; (b) processing is NECESSARY (not speculative); (c) the individual's interests do not override yours (the "balancing test"). [Derrick]
3. **First-contact disclosure mandatory**: company identity + processing purpose + legal basis + data source + prospect's rights (access / rectification / objection / erasure) + working unsubscribe link. [Derrick]
4. **Data minimization**: only enrich + contact prospects WHEN buying-intent signal is present; do NOT speculatively store data on thousands of cold contacts. [Unify + GrowthSpree]
5. **Signal-based prospecting > mass-list purchasing**. The 4 highest-converting B2B SaaS signals 2026: (a) new VP/Director/C-level hires in buying function (90-day budget mandate); (b) competitor renewal windows (60-90 days before contract end); (c) category-keyword intent surges + first-party content visits; (d) recent funding rounds. **3+ signals in alignment = high-confidence buying intent.** [GrowthSpree]
6. **Suppression-list across ALL systems** (CRM + email tools + enrichment providers) for erasure requests. [Derrick + Unify]

**Holistika alignment**: `GOI_POI_STANCE_DOCTRINE.md` (active) already operationalizes principles 1, 2, 4, and partially 5 (the Holistika doctrine names "bounded research per POI" + "only research what's needed for the engagement"). Principles 3 (first-contact disclosure) and 6 (suppression-list cross-system) are **NOT yet explicitly named in the canonical** — these are the 2 reinforcements that should land at C2.

**Crucial framing claim (load-bearing for investor brief):** The operator's *"not that much that will result creepy"* framing is the FOUNDER-ETHICAL position that the GDPR architecture mechanically enforces. The convergence of operator-ethics + industry-compliance + framework-architecture into a single doctrine is a Holistika differentiator that investors (especially Type-A High-Craft + Type-D Operational-Trust) read as evidence of operational maturity.

## Insights extracted (rated + ranked)

### Insight G-1 — "Legitimate interest under GDPR with 3 cumulative conditions" (Derrick + Instantly + Unify; CL4-HIGH; RANK 1)

**Verbatim claim** (Derrick): *"GDPR allows B2B data enrichment under legitimate interest without prior consent, but requires: informing prospects on first contact, offering a simple opt-out, keeping data for a maximum of 3 years, and documenting all processing activities."*

**Why this matters for Holistika:** Validates the legal framework for the operator's bounded-intelligence approach. The 3 conditions (relevance + necessity + balancing) are operationally identical to the Holistika doctrine's "only research what's needed for the engagement" position. Reinforcement needed at C2: add to `GOI_POI_STANCE_DOCTRINE.md` an explicit §"GDPR legal basis" section naming legitimate interest + the 3-condition test + the LIA documentation requirement.

### Insight G-2 — "Signal-based > mass-list (data minimization by design)" (Unify + GrowthSpree; CL3-HIGH; RANK 1)

**Verbatim claim** (Unify): *"Signal-based targeting flips this model. Instead of starting with a massive list and hoping some fraction is relevant, you start with buying signals, such as website visits, job postings, technology adoption, or content engagement, and only enrich and contact prospects who show active intent. This approach reduces the total volume of personal data you process, which is a core principle of GDPR's data minimization requirement under Article 5(1)(c)."*

**Why this matters for Holistika:** Validates the bounded-intelligence stance NOT just ethically but legally — minimal-data approach IS the GDPR-compliant approach. The Holistika doctrine should explicitly cite the data-minimization GDPR Article 5(1)(c) basis. This converts a values-based framing ("not creepy") into a compliance-grounded framing ("GDPR-mandated data minimization").

### Insight G-3 — "4 highest-converting B2B SaaS signals + 3+-signal-alignment heuristic" (GrowthSpree; CL3-HIGH; RANK 2)

**Verbatim claim**: *"The four highest-converting signals for B2B SaaS in 2026 are: new VP, Director, or C-level hires in the buying function (90-day budget mandate), competitor renewal windows (typically 60–90 days before contract end), category-keyword intent surges combined with first-party content visits, and recent funding rounds for the target account. Each signal alone is noise; three or more in alignment produces a high-confidence buying-intent score."*

**Why this matters for Holistika:** Provides the substrate for a future `INTELLIGENCEOPS_REGISTER.csv` signal-taxonomy column (if not already present). For the Type-A High-Craft + Type-D Operational-Trust investor briefs, the operator can cite this as the OUTREACH-discipline that prevents waste of operator + partner-time. Defer signal-taxonomy registry to a future MKTOPS-paired-runbook activation (C3 commit).

### Insight G-4 — "Champion alone cannot close — L5 buying-group visibility" (GrowthSpree; CL3-MEDIUM; RANK 3)

**Verbatim claim**: *"A common failure mode: an enthusiastic champion (usually a Director-level user) has 18 touchpoints over six weeks, but the economic buyer (usually a VP or C-suite) has zero. Without L5 visibility, this looks like a 'hot account' — and it isn't."*

**Why this matters for Holistika:** Validates the buying-group-completeness requirement from Prong B's lifecycle taxonomy. Every Holistika engagement needs ≥3 POIs mapped (champion + economic-buyer + technical-evaluator at minimum). The `GOI_POI_REGISTER.csv` already supports this; the lifecycle taxonomy (Prong B Insight B-2) reinforces it; this prong validates the industry-evidence for the requirement.

### Insight G-5 — "Suppression-list cross-system + 3-year retention max" (Derrick; CL4-MEDIUM; RANK 3)

**Verbatim claim** (Derrick): *"keep data for a maximum of 3 years."*

**Verbatim claim** (Unify): *"If a prospect exercises their right to erasure under Article 17, you must be able to honor that request across every system that holds their data, including your CRM, email tools, and enrichment providers."*

**Why this matters for Holistika:** Currently the Holistika `GOI_POI_REGISTER.csv` does NOT carry a retention-date column or a suppression-flag column. Both are needed for GDPR compliance at any meaningful scale. C2 amendment to the canonical should NAME this gap explicitly + forward-charter the schema amendment to a future canonical-CSV mint wave (NOT at C2 because the actual schema change is itself a CSV gate per `akos-governance-remediation.mdc`).

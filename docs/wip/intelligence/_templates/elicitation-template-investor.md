Reliability codes (adapted from FM 2-22.3 §B-12):

- **A** — completely reliable (verified from multiple independent sources)
- **B** — usually reliable (verified from one independent source)
- **C** — fairly reliable (single source, plausible)
- **D** — not usually reliable (single source, contradictory evidence elsewhere)
- **E** — unreliable (source has demonstrated reverse-narrative motive)
- **F** — cannot be judged (insufficient evidence to grade)

Credibility codes (adapted from FM 2-22.3 §B-13):

- **1** — confirmed by other sources
- **2** — probably true (logically consistent with other accepted facts)
- **3** — possibly true (logically possible but unconfirmed)
- **4** — doubtful (logically possible but contradictory to other accepted facts)
- **5** — improbable (contradictory to multiple other accepted facts)
- **6** — cannot be judged

## Discovery questions (in approximate order)

### Section A — Frame-setting (no extraction; signals our discipline)

1. `[Acknowledge thesis]` — "We've read [public-thesis-statement]; want to confirm we understand the shape correctly."
2. `[Invitation to share recent reflections]` — "What has the last [12-18-24] months changed in how you frame [sector / stage / geo]?"

### Section B — Direct elicitation (extracts the counterparty's current operating thesis)

3. What's an investment from the last 12 months that surprised you in execution — positive or negative?
4. When you walk into a partnership IC for a Series A, what's the question you find yourself returning to?
5. What's the failure mode you see most often in [sector] founders at our stage?

### Section C — Indirect elicitation (extracts the counterparty's view of us by reflection)

6. Of the [companies in their portfolio at our stage], who do you think we'd benchmark closest to — and where would we have to differ?
7. What's a portfolio company you backed that you'd describe as "right hypothesis, wrong moment"?

### Section D — Reverse-elicitation (signals competence; extracts disqualification signals)

8. We've identified [3 specific risks] in our trajectory. Which one would we have to retire before you'd write a cheque?
9. We've explicitly chosen *not* to do [specific anti-pattern]; in your experience, does that align or misalign with how you think about this stage?

### Section E — Closing (extract residual concerns and lock the next step)

10. What would you need to see in a follow-up to feel confident allocating diligence time?

## Post-engagement reporting

Produce an intelligence report (per SOP-IO_INTELLIGENCE_REPORT_001) with:

- **BLUF (1 sentence)** — counterparty's likely posture toward a follow-up.
- **Source-graded reliability assessment** of any new claims the counterparty made.
- **Updated baseline reality assessment** — what changed in our model of this counterparty.
- **Recommended next action** — proceed to follow-up / pause / disengage.
- **Translated external register** — the rendered version that becomes our deck companion or follow-up email.

The intelligence report is internal-register; the translated version is the external-register render.

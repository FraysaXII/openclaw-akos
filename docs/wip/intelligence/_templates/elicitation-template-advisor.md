## Discovery questions

### Section A — Frame-setting (no extraction; signals discipline)

1. *Acknowledge their background* — "We've read [public-work]; want to confirm we understand the throughline correctly."
2. *Invitation* — "What's a recent advising relationship that surprised you in execution — positive or negative?"

### Section B — Direct elicitation

3. When you advise companies at our stage, what's the question you find yourself returning to?
4. What's the failure mode you see most often in [our sector] founders?
5. How do you balance giving us your honest read vs. respecting our agency over the call?

### Section C — Indirect elicitation

6. Of the companies you've advised who reached our stage, who do you think we'd benchmark closest to — and where would we have to differ?
7. What's a piece of advice you gave that turned out wrong — and what made it wrong?

### Section D — Reverse-elicitation

8. We've identified [3 specific risks]. Which one would you push us to retire first?
9. We've explicitly chosen *not* to do [specific anti-pattern]; in your experience, does that align or misalign with how you think about this stage?
10. What would make this an advising relationship you'd disengage from?

### Section E — Closing

11. What cadence would feel right for our next interaction — and what do you want to see from us before that?

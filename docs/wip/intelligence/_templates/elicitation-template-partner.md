# Elicitation template — Partner / collaborator counterparty

> Internal working template for partner-fit engagements (channel partners, integration partners, advisor-firms partnering with us, complementary-service partnerships). Partner engagements are **bidirectional discovery** — both sides are evaluating fit simultaneously.

## Counterparty baseline reality assessment

| Field | Value |
|:---|:---|
| Counterparty name (anonymised) | `[Partner-NN]` |
| Partnership type | channel / integration / co-marketing / referral / capability-bridging / strategic |
| Counterparty maturity | early / mid-stage / mature |
| Mutual-economic-overlap | high (we serve same buyer) / medium (adjacent) / low (different but compatible) |
| Decision-shape | sole-partner-lead / executive-team / mutual-CEO |
| Engagement appetite (declared) | what they say they want from this |
| Engagement appetite (inferred) | from observed past partnerships + structural business needs |
| Capability gap they're filling (their view) | what they think we provide that they lack |
| Capability gap they're filling (our view) | what we actually need from them |
| Past-partnership patterns | what kinds of partnerships they've started; what they've shut down |
| Anti-patterns | partnerships they've publicly walked away from + reasons given |

## Approach techniques (planned)

| Technique | Specific to partners | Rationale |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Direct interrogation | NO | Damages bidirectional rapport |
| Direct elicitation | YES — primary | Partner conversations expect mutual probing |
| Indirect elicitation | YES — useful | Reveals their view of us via comparators |
| Provocation | OCCASIONALLY | Acceptable when testing a structural-fit concern; never opener |

## Source grading

Partner-side sources: company website + investor materials, partner-specific public statements, mutual-customer chatter (with consent), partner-team's individual public profiles.

## Discovery questions

### Section A — Frame-setting

1. *Mutual context* — "Here's how we got here [our path], here's how you got here [their path]; let's confirm we're seeing the same picture of what's at the intersection."
2. *Open invitation* — "What does a partnership that worked **really well** look like for you — and what made it work?"

### Section B — Direct elicitation

3. What's the last partnership you initiated that surprised you — positive or negative?
4. When you evaluate a partner like us, what's the question you find yourself returning to?
5. Where in your business does an external partner most often fall short of expectations?

### Section C — Indirect elicitation

6. Of the partnerships you've had at our maturity level, who'd you say we'd benchmark closest to — and where would we have to differ?
7. What's a partnership that looked promising on paper but didn't deliver — what was the gap?

### Section D — Reverse-elicitation

8. We've identified [structural-fit risks] in this potential partnership. Which one would we have to retire before you'd commit?
9. We've explicitly chosen *not* to pursue [partnership-pattern]; in your experience, does that align or misalign with how you think about partnerships?
10. What partnership term would be a deal-breaker from your side?

### Section E — Closing

11. What concrete artefact (proposal / pilot / MOU / etc.) would feel right as a next step? What's the time horizon?

## Post-engagement reporting

Intelligence report focused on: BLUF on partnership viability, source-graded reliability of stated commitments, mutual-fit assessment (where do we genuinely complement vs. compete), recommended next action (proceed-to-MOU / pilot-engagement / structured-evaluation / disengage).

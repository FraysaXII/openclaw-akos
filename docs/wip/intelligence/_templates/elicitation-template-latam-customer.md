# Elicitation template — LATAM customer counterparty

>
> The internal-register fields below are in English (working-language). The **discovery questions in §"Discovery questions"** are bilingual (EN canonical + ES verbatim) — the ES verbatim is what's actually said in the engagement.

## Approach techniques (planned)

Same 4 techniques as customer-SME template, with one LATAM-specific adjustment:

| Technique | LATAM-specific note |
|:---|:---|
| Direct elicitation | YES — but **with longer relationship-warmup**. Skipping warmup reads as transactional / disrespectful in many LATAM business cultures. |
| Indirect elicitation | YES — peer-anchored against **LATAM operators**, not Iberian ones; sector benchmarks should be country-appropriate. |
| Provocation | RARELY — even more cautious than customer-SME baseline; LATAM business culture tends to value face-saving. |

## Source grading

Per `SOP-IO_RELIABILITY_GRADING_001`. LATAM-specific source notes:

- **Country-specific company registers**: Mexican RPC, Colombian Cámara de Comercio, Argentine IGJ, etc. — quality varies; assume B-2 unless verified.
- **LinkedIn coverage** is generally weaker than US/EU; expect F-6 grades on individuals more often.
- **Local-press coverage** is often the best public-information source; prioritise local trade press over international.

## Discovery questions

### Section A — Frame-setting (in Spanish)

1. *Acknowledge their context* — EN: "We've looked at [public-info]; we want to confirm our understanding of where you are now." / ES: "Hemos revisado [información pública]; queremos confirmar nuestra comprensión de dónde están ustedes en este momento."
2. *Open invitation* — EN: "What has the last 6-12 months changed in how you think about [pain area]?" / ES: "¿Qué ha cambiado en los últimos 6-12 meses en cómo ustedes piensan sobre [área de dolor]?"

> Note: maintain *usted* form throughout business engagements; voseo / *vos* only if counterparty initiates and the country-context calls for it (Argentine SME founder-to-founder, for example, sometimes uses *vos* once rapport is established — follow their lead, never lead).

### Section B — Direct elicitation

3. EN: "Walk us through the last significant decision you made — what made it hard?" / ES: "¿Pueden compartir con nosotros la última decisión importante que tomaron — qué la hizo difícil?"
4. EN: "What's the day-to-day in [pain area] that loses you most time or energy?" / ES: "¿Qué del día a día en [área de dolor] les resta más tiempo o energía?"
5. EN: "When you imagine this problem solved — what does the day-to-day look like differently?" / ES: "Cuando imaginan este problema resuelto, ¿cómo se ve el día a día diferente?"

### Section C — Indirect elicitation

6. EN: "Of the operators in your sector you respect, who handles [pain area] best — and how?" / ES: "De los operadores en su sector que ustedes respetan, ¿quién maneja mejor [área de dolor] — y cómo?"
7. EN: "What advice you've gotten about this turned out not to apply to your situation?" / ES: "¿Qué consejo que han recibido sobre esto resultó no aplicar a su situación?"

### Section D — Reverse-elicitation

8. EN: "Where might we be mis-naming the friction points we've identified?" / ES: "¿En qué podríamos estar nombrando incorrectamente los puntos de fricción que hemos identificado?"
9. EN: "What's out of scope from your side — what would a successful engagement with us **not** include?" / ES: "¿Qué queda fuera de alcance desde su lado — qué **no** debería incluir una colaboración exitosa con nosotros?"
10. EN: "What's the engagement you've been pitched that you almost said yes to but didn't — and what stopped you?" / ES: "¿Qué propuesta han recibido a la que casi dijeron sí pero no lo hicieron — y qué los detuvo?"

### Section E — Closing

11. EN: "If we proposed [scope], what would we have to demonstrate before you'd say yes?" / ES: "Si propusiéramos [alcance], ¿qué tendríamos que demostrar antes de que ustedes dijeran sí?"
12. EN: "What's the cadence you'd want for our next interaction?" / ES: "¿Con qué frecuencia les gustaría que nos volviéramos a comunicar?"

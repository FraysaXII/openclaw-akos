# Elicitation template — Recruiter / hiring counterparty

> Internal working template for two scenarios:
> 1. **Holistika as candidate** — operator (or team member) interviewing for an external role; we want to elicit the recruiter's actual hiring posture.
> 2. **Holistika as employer** — we are hiring; the candidate is the elicitee but the recruiter agency mediates. (Use the customer-SME or advisor templates instead when the candidate is direct.)
>
> This template covers **scenario 1**. For Holistika-as-employer (direct candidate engagement), use a hybrid of advisor-template (for senior-hire candidates) and customer-SME-template (for junior-hire structure).

## Counterparty baseline reality assessment

| Field | Value |
|:---|:---|
| Counterparty name (anonymised) | `[Recruiter-NN]` (recruiter individual) + `[Firm-NN]` (recruiter firm) |
| Recruiter type | retained-search / contingent / in-house-talent / executive-search / specialist-domain |
| Domain specialisation | `[sector + seniority-band + geography]` |
| Engagement type | active-search-pull (specific role they want to fill) / pipeline-building (general candidate-relationship) |
| Compensation structure (declared) | how the recruiter is paid (we usually don't know exactly; inferred from recruiter type) |
| Decision-criteria for candidate-fit (declared) | what they say they're looking for |
| Decision-criteria (inferred) | from observed past placements + their public-profile patterns |
| Time-to-fill urgency | active-acute / active-paced / pipeline-only |
| Anti-patterns (declared) | candidates / situations they say they don't pursue |
| Anti-patterns (inferred) | from past walkaways + industry-typical concerns |

## Approach techniques (planned)

| Technique | Specific to recruiters | Rationale |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Direct interrogation | NO | Reads as candidate-paranoia; damages rapport |
| Direct elicitation | YES — primary | Recruiters expect substantive candidate engagement |
| Indirect elicitation | YES — useful | Reveals their actual fit-criteria via comparators (other candidates they've placed) |
| Provocation | NEVER | Recruiter relationships are typically transactional; provocation reads as candidate-instability |

## Source grading

Recruiter-side sources: their LinkedIn (placements pattern), their firm's public placements history, their published pieces (if any), mutual-network intelligence on prior-candidate experience.

## Discovery questions (we're being elicited for fit; this section captures **what we want to learn FROM the recruiter** to evaluate the role on our side)

### Section A — Frame-setting

1. *Confirm scope* — "Help us confirm we understand the role: title [X], compensation [band Y], reporting [Z], geography [W], time-discipline [hire-by-when]."
2. *Open invitation* — "What's the recent placement you made that you're most pleased with — and what made it work?"

### Section B — Direct elicitation

3. When you evaluate candidates at this seniority level, what's the question you find yourself returning to?
4. What's the failure mode you see most often in candidates for this role-type?
5. What's the **counterparty's** [employer's] biggest concern about this hire that you've heard them articulate?

### Section C — Indirect elicitation

6. Of the candidates you've placed at this level, who do you think we'd benchmark closest to — and where would we have to differ?
7. What's a placement that looked promising but didn't work out — what was the misalignment?

### Section D — Reverse-elicitation

8. We've identified [3 fit-concerns] from our side. Which one would the employer side push back on?
9. We've explicitly **not** done [common-pattern]; in your view, does that align or misalign with what the employer wants?
10. What about this role would be a deal-breaker from a candidate side that you'd want us to know up front?

### Section E — Closing

11. What's the timeline + cadence for next steps? When do we hear back, and what would make the next step more / less likely?

## Post-engagement reporting

Intelligence report focused on: BLUF on role-fit viability, source-graded reliability of recruiter-stated employer concerns (recruiters sometimes reframe; cross-grade against any direct-employer signals if available), updated baseline reality on the recruiter individual + firm, recommended next action (proceed-to-employer-direct / withdraw / pace-down).

**For Holistika-as-employer scenarios** (using a different template), the post-engagement report covers candidate-fit assessment + pipeline-fit + offer-decision recommendation.

# Elicitation template — Customer-SME counterparty

> Internal working template for SME customer discovery (Spanish or international SME; if Spanish, see also LATAM template variant). Customer-SME engagements are **operationally the most frequent** for Holistika; the elicitation discipline here is the workhorse.

## Approach techniques (planned)

| Technique | Specific to SME | Rationale |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Direct interrogation | NO | Reads as adversarial in commercial discovery |
| Direct elicitation | YES — primary | SME founders expect substantive discovery in exchange for their time |
| Indirect elicitation (peer-anchored) | YES — useful | "Other operators in your sector have told us …" surfaces unstated concerns |
| Provocation | RARELY | Sometimes useful in re-engagement when the SME has been ambivalent |

## Source grading

SME-side sources: company website, LinkedIn (operator + key team), Companies House / national-register (filing patterns, latest accounts), industry-press coverage (if any), mutual-network intelligence.

## Discovery questions

### Section A — Frame-setting

1. *Acknowledge their context* — "We've looked at [public-information]; we want to confirm our understanding of where you are right now."
2. *Open invitation* — "What's something the last [6-12] months have changed in how you think about [their stated pain area]?"

### Section B — Direct elicitation

3. Walk us through the last [significant decision] you made — what made it hard, what made it doable?
4. What's the day-to-day in [their pain area] that loses you most time or energy?
5. When you imagine this problem solved — what does the day-to-day look like differently?

### Section C — Indirect elicitation

6. Of the operators in your sector you respect, who do you think handles [pain area] best — and how?
7. What's a piece of advice you've gotten about this that turned out not to apply to your situation?

### Section D — Reverse-elicitation

8. Where are the [3 friction points] we've identified at risk of mis-naming — i.e., where would you push back?
9. What would a successful engagement with us **not** include? What's out of scope from your side?
10. What's the engagement you've been pitched that you almost said yes to but didn't — and what stopped you?

### Section E — Closing

11. If we proposed [scope shape], what would we have to demonstrate or commit to before you'd say yes?
12. What's the cadence you'd want for our next interaction?

## Post-engagement reporting

Intelligence report (per SOP-IO_INTELLIGENCE_REPORT_001): BLUF on engagement viability + recommended scope (per `SERVICE_OFFERING_CATALOG.md` cells) + source-graded confidence on stated pain + recommended next action (proceed-to-proposal / discovery-extend / disengage).
